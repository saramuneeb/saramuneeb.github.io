I am a PhD candidate at the Centre for Robotics, Queensland University of Technology (QUT), Brisbane, where my research focuses on assistive technology frameworks for workers with intellectual disabilities. I hold an MPhil in User Experience / Computer Science from QUT, and have over a decade of combined experience in UX research, human-computer interaction, and university teaching.
My research sits at the intersection of human-robot interaction, participatory design, and disability studies. I am particularly interested in understanding how assistive and emerging technologies can be designed to enhance the autonomy of workers with intellectual disabilities in professional workplace settings, while reducing the workload placed on their support providers.

Background
Before joining QUT, I was a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science at COMSATS University, Lahore (2015–2023), where I taught Human-Computer Interaction and supervised undergraduate final-year projects. I also worked as a UX Researcher at the Research Centre for HCI, COMSATS University, and as a Research Assistant at the Innovation for Poverty Alleviation Lab, Information Technology University (ITU), Lahore.
In 2023 I completed a UX Research internship at Clinical Excellence Queensland, conducting usability testing and co-design workshops for a kidney care digital health tool. More recently, in 2025, I conducted qualitative field research with Multicap–VertaView, investigating how assistive technologies can be leveraged in workplaces to enhance the autonomy of workers with intellectual disabilities.
I am also a Sessional Tutor in the Faculty of Science at QUT, and have previously tutored at the University of Queensland (DSGN1200 – Design: Experience).
